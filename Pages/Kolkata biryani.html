How to Make the Ultimate Fluffy and Fragrant Biryani

Kolkata Biriyani

Kolkata Biryani is a distinguished and beloved variant of biryani, deeply rooted in the rich culinary heritage of Bengal. This dish is renowned for its delicate balance of flavors, fragrant aroma, and the unique addition of potatoes, which sets it apart from other regional biryanis.

The hallmark of Kolkata Biryani is its subtle and aromatic nature. The basmati rice is cooked with whole spices like bay leaves, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon, creating a fragrant base. Tender pieces of marinated meat, typically mutton or chicken, are layered with the rice, along with boiled eggs and large chunks of potatoes that have been fried to a golden crispness. The potatoes, considered a luxurious addition during the Nawab’s time, remain an integral part of the dish, providing a rich and comforting texture.


Ingredients

Here’s a detailed recipe for preparing Kolkata Biryani, known for its delicate flavors, fragrant aroma, and the signature addition of potatoes. This recipe serves 4-6 people.

  1. For the Rice:
    • Basmati rice: 3 cups
    • Water: 6 cups
    • Bay leaves: 2
    • Green cardamom: 4
    • Cloves: 4
    • Cinnamon stick: 1 small piece
    • Salt: to taste
  2. For the Meat Marinade:
    • Mutton (goat meat) or chicken: 1 kg, cut into medium-sized pieces
    • Yogurt: 1 cup
    • Ginger-garlic paste: 2 tablespoons
    • Red chili powder: 1 teaspoon
    • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon
    • Garam masala powder: 1 teaspoon
    • Fried onions (Birista): 1 cup (from 2-3 medium onions)
    • Salt: to taste
    • Mustard oil: 3 tablespoons
    • Ghee (clarified butter): 2 tablespoons
    • Potatoes: 3-4 medium, peeled and halved
    • Eggs: 4, hard-boiled and shelled
    • Saffron: a pinch, soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk
    • Kewra water: 1 tablespoon
    • Rose water: 1 tablespoon
    • Biryani masala (store-bought or homemade): 1 tablespoon
    • Fresh coriander leaves: chopped, for garnish

Kolkata Biriyani Preparation

  1. Marinate the Meat:
    • In a large bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, salt, and half of the fried onions.
      Add the mutton or chicken to this mixture, ensuring the meat is well coated. Cover and let it marinate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. Cook the Rice:
    • Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
    • In a large pot, bring 6-7 cups of water to a boil. Add bay leaves, green cardamom, cloves, cinnamon stick, and salt.
    • Add the rice and cook until it is about 70% done (the grains should be soft but still firm). Drain the rice and set it aside.
  3. Prepare the Potatoes:
    • Heat mustard oil in a pan until it smokes slightly. Fry the halved potatoes until they are golden brown on the outside. Remove and set aside.
    • In the same oil, fry the boiled eggs until they have a light golden crust. Set them aside.
  4. Cook the Marinated Meat:
    • In the same pan, add a bit more oil or ghee if needed. Add the marinated meat along with any remaining marinade. Cook on medium heat until the meat is
      tender and the oil begins to separate from the gravy. This may take 30-40 minutes for mutton and less time for chicken.
  5. Layering the Biryani:
    • In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, spread a layer of cooked rice at the bottom.
    • Add a layer of the cooked meat along with some of its gravy.
    • Place a few fried potatoes and eggs on top.
    • Sprinkle some biryani masala, the remaining fried onions, and a few drops of kewra water, rose water, and saffron milk.
    • Repeat the layers until all the rice, meat, potatoes, and eggs are used, finishing with a layer of rice on top.
  6. Dum Cooking:
    • Seal the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can seal the edges with dough to trap the steam inside.
    • Place the pot on low heat and cook for 30-40 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. You can place a heavy tawa (griddle) under the pot to
      distribute the heat evenly and prevent burning.
  7. Serve:
